## 5.1 Global Reach and the Limits of Fidelity
Previous chapters established the 35-metric compass (Chapter 2) as a diagnostic lens that converts public evidence into 2D phase-space coordinates:
- **X-axis**: economic metabolism (left/collectivist to right/market tilt)
- **Y-axis**: governance density (libertarian to authoritarian/suppressive)

The 15×6 Master Grid then maps interaction dominance across the six evolutionarily stable moves (Mutualism, Commensalism, Parasitism, Competition, Amensalism, Neutralism) for each of the 15 base rules. Rule 13 (Cheater Detection & Suppression) is the critical hinge: >30% parasitism share in Row 13 correlates with reduced longevity, open to empirical stress-testing.

Chapters 3 and 4 used this protocol to benchmark biological superorganisms (ants baseline: near-perfect fidelity via hardware) and small-scale institutional approximations (Venice plateau: sustainable via short-lag rotation and overlapping councils). Both cases show high mutualism dominance and low Rule-13 parasitism when scale and lag are minimal.

Chapter 5 turns to the first true global replicator: the Dutch East India Company (VOC, 1602–1799). The VOC operated across oceanic distances, fusing joint-stock capital, quasi-sovereign powers, and trade networks into a far-from-equilibrium system far larger than any prior polity or corporation. It is the ideal test case for the central question of global scaling: **how does distance erode fidelity, amplify cheater bandwidth, and raise suppression costs until entropy export fails?**

The ensemble consensus scoring (averaged from four LLMs) produces the baseline lattice (Fig 5.2) and a shocked simulation of the 1780s Fourth Anglo-Dutch War (Fig 5.3), revealing the thermodynamic signature of a high-reach replicator under stress.

## 5.2 VOC Global Scaling – The Lattice Fracture and Terminal Shock
The VOC fused joint-stock capital, quasi-sovereign powers, and global trade into a far-from-equilibrium replicator of unprecedented reach. The baseline lattice (Fig 5.2) projects coherence: ~95% mutualism dominance across most base rules, supported by spice monopolies, naval boundaries, and information storage (G3).

**Primary thermodynamic scar – Row 13 (Cheater Detection & Suppression)**  
50.5% mutualism vs 49.5% parasitism. The Rule-13 proxy of 47.1% exceeds the collapse threshold (>30%). The red border and note ("Critical Rule 13: >30 % Parasitism → Potential Collapse Risk") highlight this hinge. The fracture is driven by **entropy lag**: institutional pheromones (orders, audits) decayed over six-month Amsterdam–Batavia voyages, eroding real-time detection and turning Rule 13 into a high-entropy channel. Historical records of endemic private trade, embezzlement, and "vergaan onder corruptie" align directly.

**Secondary fracture – Row 3 (Heredity Fidelity / Allow Heritable Variation)**  
63.2% mutualism vs 36.8% parasitism. Parasitic drain into administrative continuity — nepotism in the Heeren XVII, corruption dynasties in colonial posts — degraded long-term replicator coherence.

Compass coordinates: X = 0.25 (modest positive economic metabolism, buoyed by profit motive and niche construction but tempered by monopoly/parasitism drag), Y = -1.00 (emerging governance density from military priority, dissent suppression, and ethnic hierarchies). Zone 2 places VOC in a balanced but fragile attractor. Estimated longevity window (~111–207 years) brackets the actual 197-year arc, with the upper bound optimistic given compounding leakage.

Contrast with the ant baseline (near-100% mutualism, Rule-13 ~98%): biological superorganisms achieve near-perfect fidelity through innate hardware. VOC trades fidelity for reach, sustaining higher parasitism through continuous external recruitment — but the fractures show the buffer is finite.

The VOC arc — baseline fracture to shocked kill-switch — marks the thermodynamic ceiling of pre-digital replicators. Oceanic distances created unavoidable entropy lag: six-month round-trip signaling decayed Rule-13 fidelity, forcing the system to tolerate ~47–60% parasitism in its critical suppression hinge. External recruitment buffers (capital, labor, colonies) delayed collapse, but could not eliminate the compounding leak. When war overloaded metabolism, the ε-ratio crossed 1.5, suppression costs overwhelmed export capacity, and the system entered terminal descent.

This fragility is not unique to the VOC; it is structural to any replicator whose coordination bandwidth is limited by physical distance and human-scale signaling. Biological superorganisms (ants) solve lag with chemical hardware; small-scale polities (Venice) solve it with overlapping councils and short physical loops. Global corporate replicators like VOC are forced to live with the lag — and pay the metabolic price.

Digital systems change the equation fundamentally. Entropy lag drops from months to milliseconds. Coordination becomes near-instantaneous, potentially healing Rule-13 fractures that plagued the VOC. But scale does not disappear — it explodes. The question is no longer “can suppression keep pace with distance?” but “what new forms of parasitism emerge when coordination is instantaneous and the replicator is planetary in reach?”

**Chapter 6** explores this new regime: digital replicators operating at network speed, with near-zero lag but unprecedented variation bandwidth and attack surface. Does millisecond coordination produce synthetic superorganisms with unprecedented fidelity? Or does it simply relocate the Rule-13 fracture — from physical distance to algorithmic exploitation, information warfare, and emergent coordination failures? The lattice suggests fidelity remains the hinge — only the medium has changed.
