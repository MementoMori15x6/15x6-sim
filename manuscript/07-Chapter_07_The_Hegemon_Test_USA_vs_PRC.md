Nation-states are the current apex of far-from-equilibrium replicating systems: planetary agent counts, global energetic coupling, informational flows spanning continents. The United States (post-1971 dollar-reserve era) and the People's Republic of China (post-1978 Reform & Opening era) function as high-fidelity, high-metabolism hegemons — each exporting entropy at unprecedented scale while attempting to suppress internal cheaters and external rivals.

The central question at this scale is stark:  
Can a replicator reach planetary hegemony while keeping Rule-13 parasitism <30% **and** preserving high adaptive variation bandwidth — or is the thermodynamic tension between cheat-resistance and rigidity unavoidable?

Two competing strategies emerge:

**Soft Entropy Export (USA)**  
Market vitality and institutional modularity allow entropy to swirl outward (dollar reserve sink, lobbying leakage buffered by innovation/resilience). Drift is gradual but persistent.

**Hard Entropy Export (PRC)**  
Centralized suppression and infrastructure dominance build a high-pressure containment vessel (Social Credit, BRI). Control is tight but brittle — signal cascades and rigidity can fracture suddenly.

### 7.5 The Hegemon Fork – Soft vs. Hard Entropy Export
At planetary scale, the replicator must export entropy globally while suppressing internal cheaters. The two strategies trade off differently:
- Soft → high variation bandwidth but accumulating drift
- Hard → low drift but collapsing variation bandwidth

Neither maintains Rule-13 <30% **and** C2 >5–6 indefinitely. The planetary replicator configuration itself becomes the thermodynamic trap.
