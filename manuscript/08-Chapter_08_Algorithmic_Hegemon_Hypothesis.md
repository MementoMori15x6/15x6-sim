## 8.1 The Dead-End Trap at Planetary Scale

Chapter 07 tested the two current planetary replicators (USA and PRC) against modest shocks (de-dollarization, rigidity escalation). Both crossed the Rule-13 parasitism threshold (>30%) in baseline or under stress, resulting in longevity compression from centuries to decades.

This revealed a thermodynamic dead-end at global scale:
- Centralized entropy export channels (dollar hegemony, BRI corridors) create single-point fragility.
- Suppression costs (governance density Y) rise to contain parasitism, eroding variation bandwidth (C2) and accelerating brittleness.
- The parasitism-adaptability tension becomes unsolvable without fracturing the replicator.

The question carrying into Chapter 08:  
If neither hegemon can resolve the trap at planetary scale, is the planetary replicator itself a dead-end configuration? Or is a hybrid digital-state replicator — an **Algorithmic Hegemon** — the thermodynamically viable successor?

## 8.2 Hypothesis & Test Protocol

The Algorithmic Hegemon hypothesis posits a successor that grafts decentralized digital protocols (consensus, automated detection) onto state machinery to:
- Distribute entropy sinks (multi-chain oracles, sovereign modules)
- Outsource Rule-13 suppression to code (slashing, anomaly flagging)
- Preserve variation bandwidth under stress (C2 >7)

We test early prototypes against explicit successor criteria (Ch. 08 goals):
- Rule-13 proxy <20% baseline, <30% post-shock
- Variation bandwidth (C2 >7) preserved
- Longevity window centuries (≥500 years ±50%)
- Rigidity penalty low (Y near 0 or mildly positive)
- Distributed entropy sinks (≥3 independent channels)
- ε-Ratio <1.0 long-term

Protocol:
- Baseline: 35-metric compass on public 2026 data
- Shock: Institutional capture (+10–15% delta on D1 parasitism & G1 cheater detection)
- Diagnostics: compass coords, Rule-13 proxy, longevity estimate, Row 13 intensity in the 15×6 grid

### Diagnostic Reading
All three digital replicators extend baseline longevity (centuries vs. nation-state decades) and variation bandwidth compared to paper-ware substrates (Ch. 07). Yet under modest capture stress they converge on the same fracture: Rule-13 proxy >30%, Row 13 parasitism dominance, longevity compression ~33–38%.

While Rome’s parasitism was geographic (frontier tax farming), the VOC’s oceanic (shareholder lag), Ethereum’s and Polkadot’s is stake-based (validation concentration). The AI-hegemon variant demonstrates tactical advantage — better detection lowers baseline proxy to 19.0% and Row 13 parasitism to 18.6% — but fails to prevent shock-induced phase transition.

To escape the trap, a replicator must not only detect cheaters (AI-G1) but also distribute metabolic waste (entropy) across multiple independent sub-lattices (**Fractal Sinks**) so no single capture vector can trigger total failure.
