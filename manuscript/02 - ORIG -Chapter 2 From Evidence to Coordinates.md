### Chapter 2: From Evidence to Coordinates: The Reproducible Protocol

#### 2.1 Purpose of this chapter

The board introduced in Chapter 1 is not a metaphor; it is a measurable phase space. The present chapter transforms it into a scientific instrument by specifying, step by step and without ambiguity, how any political-economic organism—individual, corporation, movement, or state—is converted from raw historical evidence into (a) exact X,Y coordinates on the 15×6 grid and (b) a deterministic heat-map percentage distribution across the ten canonical zones. Every calculation that follows is fully reproducible by any researcher equipped with a calculator and public sources.

#### 2.3 From raw metric scores to exact X,Y coordinates

Economic X = weighted average of groups A–D
Social/Governance Y = weighted average of groups E–H (I–K applied only when |X| or |Y| falls within ±0.3 of a border).

Example (Apple Inc., 2025):
Economic raw = +4.82 → final X = **+4.80**
Social raw = –5.41 → final Y = **–5.40**

#### 2.5 The percentage formula (exact and final)

For an organism at coordinates (X₀, Y₀):

1. Compute Euclidean distance dᵢ to each of the ten zone centers.
2. Raw weight wᵢ = 1 / (dᵢ² + 0.3)
3. Percentage for zone i = (wᵢ / Σw) × 100

The constant 0.3 prevents division by zero and guarantees every zone receives at least trace probability.

#### 2.6 Edge cases and boundary rules

- Any coordinate beyond ±8.0 on either axis triggers “Far/Extreme” bleed: the excess distance is capped and the three closest zones share the overflow proportionally.
- Rule 13 (“80 % parasitism”) is a convenience label applied post-calculation when Y ≥ +8.0 and >75 % of GDP is extracted via non-market coercion. It does not alter the mathematics.
