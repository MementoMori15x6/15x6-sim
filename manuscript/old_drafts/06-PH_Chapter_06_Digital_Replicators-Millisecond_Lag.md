## 6.1 From Months to Milliseconds – The Regime Change

Chapter 5 showed that oceanic distance created unavoidable entropy lag: six-month round-trip signaling decayed Rule-13 fidelity in the VOC, forcing the system to tolerate 47–60% parasitism in its critical suppression hinge. External recruitment buffers delayed collapse, but could not eliminate the compounding leak. When war overloaded metabolism, the ε-ratio crossed 1.5, suppression costs overwhelmed export capacity, and the system entered terminal descent.

Digital systems flip the lag variable to near-zero. Coordination becomes instantaneous across planetary scale. The question is no longer “can suppression keep pace with distance?” but “what new forms of parasitism emerge when coordination is instantaneous and the replicator is planetary in reach?”

## 6.2 Defining Digital Replicators

Digital replicators are far-from-equilibrium systems whose heredity, variation, persistence, and interaction moves are encoded and executed primarily in software, networks, and algorithms. Examples include:
- Open-source protocols and DAOs (on-chain governance)
- Algorithmic trading swarms and high-frequency markets
- Meme-coin / social-token ecosystems
- AI agent networks (autonomous decision loops)
- Large-scale social platforms (coordination via feeds, virality, moderation)

Key regime shift:
- Lag: milliseconds instead of months
- Variation bandwidth: near-infinite (forks, smart contracts, code updates)
- Attack surface: exponentially larger (51% attacks, sybil, flash-loan exploits, memetic hijack)
- Recruitment: global, permissionless, near-costless

## 6.3 Placeholder Questions for the Lattice

We hypothesize three possible outcomes for digital replicators:

1. **Synthetic Superorganism**  
   Near-zero lag + modular code execution heals Rule-13 fractures. Mutualism dominance approaches ant-level (~98%+), longevity extends dramatically. Systems self-heal via on-chain audits, reputation tokens, slashing mechanisms.

2. **High-Velocity Parasitism**  
   Instant coordination amplifies cheater bandwidth. Rule-13 parasitism migrates to new vectors: flash-loan attacks, oracle manipulation, governance capture via token concentration. ε-ratio spikes faster, collapse cascades at network speed.

3. **Emergent Fracture Zones**  
   New rows become critical: information warfare (Row ? – narrative hijack), algorithmic bias (Row ? – variation capture), coordination overload (Row ? – spam/sybil). The lattice may show clean Rule 13 but fractures elsewhere.
