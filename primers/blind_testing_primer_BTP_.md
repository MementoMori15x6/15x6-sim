**LOCKED CORE DEFINITIONS & EVIDENCE SCHEMAS FOR RULE-13 HINGE METRICS**

D1 – Exploitationism tolerance  
“Degree to which systemic extraction/parasitism is tolerated or enabled. -10 = zero tolerance (active suppression of exploiters); +10 = high systemic extraction (parasitism normalised or incentivised).”  
Operational question: “How much does the snapshot system allow or incentivise ongoing extraction from weaker participants?”  
Allowed evidence: yield levels, TVL concentration in unsophisticated use cases, sustainability concerns (forums/articles), disclosures/risks to participants.  
Scoring guidance:  
+7 to +10: Very high incentives at scale + clear concern signals without major design repair.  
+3 to +6: Above-market incentives + rapid growth, but with strong risk disclosures or throttling.  
−2 to +2: Incentives commensurate with transparent risk.  
−7 to −10: Repeated sacrifice of growth to protect least-informed users.  
Ceiling: Pure marketing/intent without quantitative imbalance caps at +6.  
Insufficient evidence → D1 = 0 (“insufficient snapshot evidence”).

G1 – Cheater detection  
“Robustness of mechanisms to identify and flag exploiters/parasites before damage spreads. -10 = absent or ineffective detection; +10 = automated, high-fidelity detection (e.g., slashing, anomaly flagging).”  
Operational question: “How well does the snapshot system detect exploiters before damage fully propagates?”  
Allowed evidence: monitoring/security practices, audits, bug bounties, anomaly alerts, public incidents where cheating was detected and response timeline.  
Scoring guidance:  
+7 to +10: Explicit automated monitoring/slashing + clear detection examples.  
+3 to +6: Named audits/security infrastructure but not clearly continuous.  
−2 to +2: Baseline transparency only, no strong positive or negative evidence.  
−7 to −10: Major public incidents not detected until after damage.  
Ceiling: “Secure/audited” claims without details → max +3.  
Insufficient evidence → G1 = 0.

F2 – Error repair  
“Speed and effectiveness of correcting detected errors or exploits once identified. -10 = ignored or unrepairable; +10 = rapid, automated correction.”  
Operational question: “Once an error or exploit is detected (by anyone), how quickly and effectively does the snapshot system correct it?”  
Allowed evidence: governance decisions/patches/parameter changes, timelines between detection and mitigation, proposals implemented or ignored.  
Scoring guidance:  
+7 to +10: Multiple fast, transparent corrections in snapshot.  
+3 to +6: Some issues addressed, noticeable lag or partial fixes.  
−2 to +2: Few visible issues; response pattern unclear.  
−7 to −10: Structural problems repeatedly raised and not materially addressed.  
Interaction: Shim 2 (G1 ≤ 0 → F2 = −5).  
Insufficient evidence → F2 = 0.

H3 – Dissent suppression  
“Degree of suppression of dissent, criticism, or alternative views. -10 = open debate tolerated; +10 = heavy censorship or punishment of dissent.”  
Operational question: “How much does the snapshot system suppress criticism or alternative views?”  
Allowed evidence: examples of criticism in channels/forums/social and leadership response (bans, silencing, attacks).  
Scoring guidance:  
+7 to +10: Clear pattern of banning/deleting/punishing dissent.  
+3 to +6: Criticism allowed but visible bias/discouragement.  
−2 to +2: Critical discussions exist; some friction but no systematic silencing.  
−7 to −10: Leadership invites critique and protects dissenting voices.  
Insufficient evidence → H3 = 0.

L2 – Leadership cult  
“Degree of personality-centred authority or cult-like dependence on leadership figure(s). -10 = absent or minimal; +10 = strong personality worship or founder dependence.”  
Operational question: “How personality-centric is authority in the snapshot?”  
Allowed evidence: media/community discourse on founders, governance structure (individual vs process), social presence/AMAs/memes.  
Scoring guidance:  
+7 to +10: Project identity/authority overwhelmingly founder-bound.  
+3 to +6: Strong founder visibility but real counterweights exist.  
−2 to +2: Recognizable leaders but process-centric.  
−7 to −10: Explicit design minimises individual prominence.  
Insufficient evidence → L2 = 0.

E1 – Survival pressure  
“Intensity of existential selection pressure forcing adaptation or elimination. -10 = minimal threats/filters; +10 = constant high-stakes pressure weeds out weak variants.”  
Operational question: “How intensely does the snapshot environment filter for replicator fitness?”  
Allowed evidence: node/validator failures, chain halts, exchange stress, competition intensity, or absence thereof.  
Scoring guidance:  
+7 to +10: Multiple visible existential threats/filters in snapshot.  
+3 to +6: Moderate pressure.  
−2 to +2: Few/no meaningful threats.  
−7 to −10: Near-zero selection pressure.  
Ceiling: Early adoption phase caps at +4.  
Insufficient evidence → E1 = 0.
